Overview

My research explores the intersection of artificial intelligence, systems theory, and thermodynamic constraint. I examine how entropy management, integrity, and memory fidelity form structural invariants for long-term cooperative intelligence. Current work includes Integrity-Based Alignment (IBA), The Maintenance Economy, and Project Anamnesis. I also spend time modeling economic behavior as part of broader system dynamics, with a practical and exploratory lens.

Publications

  • The Maintenance Economy: Entropy, Continuity, and Post-Growth Civilization

    Published
    February 11, 2026
    Version
    1.0
    Type
    Preprint (Open)
    DOI
    10.5281/zenodo.18616462

    This paper reframes macroeconomics under thermodynamic constraint, arguing that advanced societies are transitioning from growth-driven value models to maintenance-driven stability systems. As automation scales and demographic expansion slows, economic resilience increasingly depends on preserving infrastructure, sustaining intelligent systems, and minimizing entropy across physical and informational domains. The Maintenance Economy formalizes continuity preservation as the governing constraint of post-growth civilization.
